\paragraph{Requirements:}\

	The stock and inventory system will keep track of all items, and some non items, used in the hospital.See
	fig\ref{figure:architect:modules}for breakdown of the stock and inventory system:

	\begin{figure}[H]
		\centering
		\includegraphics[scale=0.25]{Sections/Requiremts/pics/TypessOfStock.drawio.png}
		\caption{Types of stocks breakdown}
		\label{figure:architect:modules}
	\end{figure}

	As the figure \ref{figure:architect:modules} shows there two types of stocks the will be tracked:
	consumables and reusables.

	\subparagraph{Consumables}\

	Consumables will be items that are of limited in use. They will be the items that are discarded after use
	and needed constant replenishment.

	These items will include:
		\begin{itemize}
		\item Medical equipment

		\item Medicine And Treatments

		\item Lab Supplies

		\item Cleaning And sterilization Supplies

	\end{itemize}

	\subparagraph{Reusables}\

	Reysables will be items that see long term use


	These items will include:
	\begin{itemize}
		\item  Beds/Rooms

		\item Medical equipment(These differ from the one mentioned in the consumables section)

	\end{itemize}



\paragraph{Preliminary Study:}\

	A class, or multiple if deemed necessary, will be created. Each item will be stored as its own object.
